Hiroshima: A Symbol of Japan

Experience the iconic peak and surrounding natural beauty.

Top Attractions in Hiroshima

Hirosima Castle

Historic castle originally built in 1589, also known as “Carp Castle.” Visitors can explore the museum inside and enjoy beautiful views of the city from the top floor.

Peace Memorial Park

A peaceful park dedicated to the victims of the atomic bombing in 1945. It includes the Atomic Bomb Dome, museums, and memorial monuments honoring those who lost their lives.

Miyajima

A beautiful island famous for the floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine. Visitors can enjoy scenic views, friendly deer, hiking trails, and traditional Japanese architecture.

Miyajima Omotesando Shopping Street

A lively shopping street on Miyajima Island offering local snacks, souvenirs, and famous Hiroshima treats like grilled oysters and momiji manju.

Top restaurants in Hiroshima

Kobe Beef Yakiniku Kinjiro

A high-quality yakiniku restaurant serving premium Kobe beef. The meat is tender, rich, and grilled to perfection in a modern and stylish setting.

Food Tour

A guided food experience where visitors can explore Hiroshimas local cuisine, including okonomiyaki, fresh seafood, and traditional sweets.

Maze Men Okkundo

A popular ramen restaurant known for its flavorful maze-men (brothless noodles). The noodles are thick and chewy, topped with rich and savory ingredients.

Charcoal BBQ Buchi Namiki Branch

A traditional Japanese barbecue restaurant specializing in high-quality grilled meats cooked over charcoal for a smoky and delicious flavor.

Guttsuri-an

A cozy restaurant offering seasonal Japanese dishes made with fresh local ingredients. The meals are beautifully presented and carefully balanced in flavor.

Wine Bar ViVi

A modern wine bar with a relaxed atmosphere. It offers a wide selection of wines paired with creative Japanese dishes and small plates.
